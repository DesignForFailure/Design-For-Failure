About This Site

Design for Failure publishes independent research and analysis on topics spanning systems design, information security, programming language theory, and adjacent domains.

The work published here operates under a set of principles:

Principles

Failure is a first-class constraint. Robust systems are not built by assuming success. They are built by assuming failure at every layer and designing accordingly. The name of this site is the design philosophy itself.

Transparency over trust. Systems, arguments, and designs should be auditable. If a claim cannot be examined, it cannot be verified. If it cannot be verified, it should not be trusted.

Precision over persuasion. These publications prioritize technical accuracy and logical rigor. The goal is to present clear analysis that stands on its own merits, not to convince through rhetoric.

Function over form. Complexity must justify itself. If a simpler mechanism achieves the same guarantee, the simpler mechanism wins. Elegance is a byproduct of correct constraint identification, not an aesthetic goal.
